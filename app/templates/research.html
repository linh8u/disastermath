Several major trends became clear after sorting through the data. The number of disasters in 2010 actually matches the number that took place in 2000, but natural disasters did indeed spike in the year 2006. Also, floods and mass movement disasters appear to be increasing in frequency, whereas all others are decreasing or staying the same. Also, floods and earthquakes are the most frequent natural disasters.

Asia sees the majority of natural disasters. Also, the United States, China, Indonesia, India, and the Philippines make 30% of the world's disasters.

The differing impact among disasters also became evident. Whereas earthquakes and floods/storms kill, displace, and cause the most damage among natural disasters, more people are actually affected by wildfires, volcanoes, and droughts.
